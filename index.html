South King County Opportunity Youth

Flatiron School

This project offers an updated estimate of the number of Opportunity Youth in South King County using the 2017 5-year American Community Survey (ACS) Public Use Microdata Survey (PUMS).

In a 2016 report, nearly 19,000 youths in South King County, persons between the ages of 16 and 24, where neither at school nor are they working. Many of these youths come from impoverished communities, have issues with the law and/or have children that pose serious roadblocks to finding work or continuing in school. Project Road Map is an initiative designed to help combat this issue by helping youths tackle these problems and assist them with finding employment or schooling. Those that participated in program were 2-3 times more likely to be in school or find meaningful employment than others who weren’t able to participate.

For this project, our group of three looked at PUMS data from 2017 and attempted to model the figures given by the Project Road Map Report one year prior. We also attempted to draw meaningful insights from the data collected.
